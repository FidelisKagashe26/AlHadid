Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our programs, volunteering, and how you can get involved.

How can I volunteer with Al-Hadid Foundation?
We welcome volunteers from all backgrounds! Contact us through this form, call us directly, or visit our office to discuss opportunities that match your skills and interests. We have roles in education, healthcare support, community outreach, and administrative tasks.
What programs does the foundation currently run?
We run four main programs: Orphan Support (providing food, clothing, education, and mentorship), Women's Islamic Empowerment (education and vocational training), Mosque Development (renovation and construction), and Premature Baby Care Support (medical equipment and family assistance). Visit our Programs page to learn more about each initiative.
How are donations used and can I see financial reports?
We maintain complete transparency: 85% of donations go directly to programs, 10% to administration, and 5% to fundraising. Annual financial reports are available upon request, and we provide regular updates to donors about how their contributions are making an impact in our communities.
Can organizations and businesses partner with you?
Absolutely! We actively seek partnerships with like-minded organizations, businesses, and institutions. Whether through financial support, in-kind donations, employee volunteer programs, or collaborative projects, we welcome partnerships that amplify our impact. Contact us to discuss how we can work together.
Do you accept international volunteers and donations?
Yes, we welcome international support! We accept donations from around the world through various secure methods, and we can accommodate international volunteers for both short-term and long-term commitments. Contact us to discuss logistics and requirements for international involvement.

Ready to Make a Difference?

Join our mission to serve humanity through Islamic values of compassion and service. There are many ways to support our work and create lasting positive change.
